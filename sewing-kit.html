Sewing kit — Artist Inspiration

Still life + domestic narrative. Think: needles, thread, buttons, fabric, scissors, hands, repair, memory, care.

Concept directions

  • Symbolism: mending = healing, identity, family, labour, gender roles, tradition vs modern.
  • Texture focus: stitches, frays, denim, lace, felt, metallic scissors.
  • Composition ideas: extreme close-up, overhead flat-lay, cropped hands, dramatic single light source.

Artists to explore (starter list)

Use these as inspiration. Analyse composition, colour, light, surface, and meaning. Do not copy.

Audrey Flack

Photorealist still lifes with intense colour, reflective surfaces, symbolism.

Studio idea: Try: build a symbolic still life with 5–7 objects; paint with crisp edges + high contrast.

How to evidence this in your digital process journal
  • 1–2 slides: primary photos + quick observational sketches (label: date + location).
  • 1 slide: artist analysis (formal elements + why it links to your idea).
  • 1 slide: experiments (media tests + short evaluation: what worked / next step).
  • 1 slide: composition thumbnails + decision (why you chose one).

Starter tasks

  • Photograph your own sewing objects (10+ photos): overhead, side light, close-up texture.
  • Do 2 pages of quick observational drawings: buttons, thread spools, scissors (different angles).
  • Create a colour strip test: 6–10 mixes that match your fabric colours.
  • Make 2 thumbnail compositions: one calm/symmetrical, one dramatic/cropped.

Quick rule

Prioritise your own primary studies (photos and drawings). Secondary images are fine, but you must cite them.
