Digital process journal (digital portfolio)

Use this to present your supporting studies and final outcome clearly, in a similar format to your coursework.

Best practice (simple)

  • Photograph/scan in good light. Keep the page flat. Avoid shadows and glare.
  • Crop so the work fills the frame. Keep text readable.
  • Break A2 pages into clear sections (close-ups) so details can be seen.
  • Label each page/slide: Topic • Primary study • Experiment • Evaluation.
  • Keep writing short and useful: what you tried, what changed, what you will do next.
Suggested slide order (example)
  1. Chosen starting point + initial mind map / intentions
  2. Primary photos (your own) + observational drawings
  3. Artist research (1–2 artists) + formal analysis
  4. Media experiments (at least 2 approaches) + evaluation
  5. Composition thumbnails + decision
  6. Final plan (materials, size, steps)
  7. Final outcome photo
Quick quality checklist
  • Is it easy to understand your idea in 30 seconds?
  • Do you show development (not just one good page)?
  • Do experiments clearly influence your final choices?
  • Are sources credited for any images that are not yours?
  • Do photos show the final outcome clearly (full view + close-up)?

Helpful labels

Primary photo Observation drawing Artist analysis Experiment Evaluation Composition plan

Tip

One strong page is not enough. Show a journey: research → test → refine → resolve.

Built for idea-generation and research. Always use your own photos/drawings where possible and cite any secondary sources you use.
