Natural habitat — Artist Inspiration

Habitats can be wild, fragile, engineered, or imagined: forests, coasts, rivers, micro-habitats, urban nature.

Concept directions

  • Show scale: macro (insects/moss) vs wide landscape.
  • Communicate change: seasons, climate, pollution, human impact.
  • Atmosphere: fog, heat haze, storm light, dusk.

Artists to explore (starter list)

Use these as inspiration. Analyse composition, colour, light, surface, and meaning. Do not copy.

David Hockney

Vivid landscapes; bold colour; multiple viewpoints.

Studio idea: Try: paint a place you know using exaggerated colour and simplified shapes.

How to evidence this in your digital process journal
  • 1–2 slides: primary photos + quick observational sketches (label: date + location).
  • 1 slide: artist analysis (formal elements + why it links to your idea).
  • 1 slide: experiments (media tests + short evaluation: what worked / next step).
  • 1 slide: composition thumbnails + decision (why you chose one).

Starter tasks

  • Primary studies: 6+ photos you take yourself (plants, water, bark, stones, sky).
  • 1 page of texture experiments (dry brush, scumbling, glazing, stippling).
  • 2 tonal studies (monochrome) to plan depth and focal point.
  • 1 composition plan: foreground–midground–background with clear path for the eye.

Quick rule

Prioritise your own primary studies (photos and drawings). Secondary images are fine, but you must cite them.
