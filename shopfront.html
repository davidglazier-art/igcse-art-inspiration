Shopfront — Artist Inspiration

Shopfronts are about observation: reflections, signage, windows, products, people, time of day, and stories.

Concept directions

  • Reflections: street + interior combined (layered realities).
  • Typography: signs, neon, price labels — use as design elements.
  • Narrative: closed shop, late-night light, busy market, abandoned window.

Artists to explore (starter list)

Use these as inspiration. Analyse composition, colour, light, surface, and meaning. Do not copy.

Edward Hopper

Urban scenes; dramatic light; quiet narrative and isolation.

Studio idea: Try: paint a shop at night with strong warm/cool contrast.

How to evidence this in your digital process journal
  • 1–2 slides: primary photos + quick observational sketches (label: date + location).
  • 1 slide: artist analysis (formal elements + why it links to your idea).
  • 1 slide: experiments (media tests + short evaluation: what worked / next step).
  • 1 slide: composition thumbnails + decision (why you chose one).

Starter tasks

  • Take your own photos of 2–3 shopfronts (different times of day).
  • Sketch typography from signs (hand-drawn letterforms).
  • Make 6 thumbnails: change cropping, horizon line, and focal point.
  • Paint one small study just for reflections (glass + glare + interior).

Quick rule

Prioritise your own primary studies (photos and drawings). Secondary images are fine, but you must cite them.
