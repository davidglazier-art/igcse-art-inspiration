Gridlocked — Artist Inspiration

Gridlocked can mean traffic jams, systems, repetition, maps, or feeling trapped. Think patterns, pathways, and control.

Concept directions

  • City maps: roads as lines; junctions as nodes; cars as repeated shapes.
  • Abstract grids: order vs chaos; breaking the grid; distortion.
  • Fragmentation: split an image into tiles; shift, rotate, or blur parts.

How to evidence this in your digital process journal
  • 1–2 slides: primary photos + quick observational sketches (label: date + location).
  • 1 slide: artist analysis (formal elements + why it links to your idea).
  • 1 slide: experiments (media tests + short evaluation: what worked / next step).
  • 1 slide: composition thumbnails + decision (why you chose one).

Starter tasks

  • Primary study: take photos of traffic, crosswalks, metro maps, or parking lots.
  • Make 2 versions: one realistic, one abstracted into a grid/pattern.
  • Experiment page: masking tape lines, stencils, repeated stamps, or dry brush.
  • Create 3 composition thumbnails that show 'stuck' in different ways.

Quick rule

Prioritise your own primary studies (photos and drawings). Secondary images are fine, but you must cite them.
