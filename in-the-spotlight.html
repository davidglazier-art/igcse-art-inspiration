In the spotlight — Artist Inspiration

Spotlight = focus, performance, identity. Explore stage lighting, celebrity, vulnerability, masks, and drama.

Concept directions

  • Chiaroscuro: strong light vs deep shadow to direct attention.
  • Movement: dancers, musicians, actors — gesture and blur.
  • Concept: spotlight as pressure, judgement, fame, or self-confidence.

Artists to explore (starter list)

Use these as inspiration. Analyse composition, colour, light, surface, and meaning. Do not copy.

Caravaggio

Extreme chiaroscuro; theatrical lighting; intense realism.

Studio idea: Try: paint a portrait or still life with one strong lamp and deep shadows.

How to evidence this in your digital process journal
  • 1–2 slides: primary photos + quick observational sketches (label: date + location).
  • 1 slide: artist analysis (formal elements + why it links to your idea).
  • 1 slide: experiments (media tests + short evaluation: what worked / next step).
  • 1 slide: composition thumbnails + decision (why you chose one).

Starter tasks

  • Photograph a friend/self under a single lamp (3 angles).
  • Do 10 gesture sketches (30–60 seconds each) from performance photos.
  • Create a value plan: 5-tone scale and map it onto your composition.
  • Test 2 palettes: warm spotlight vs cool shadows; then choose one.

Quick rule

Prioritise your own primary studies (photos and drawings). Secondary images are fine, but you must cite them.
